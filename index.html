"我听到这首歌就会想到我以前家的厨房。在那个厨房里面，是烧柴火的。就是用外面剪的树枝烧火的底下做饭。上面有个锅。然后我要把那些柴塞到底下。这样你奶奶才能做饭。然后热气腾腾的做饭。然后这个灶的旁边是放着一个鸡的笼子。就是鸡就躲在旁边，晚上会回家就是住在那里。然后这个笼子的旁边，有个台子上面搭这个窝给母鸡扶那个鸡蛋，孵出小鸡来。她在扶小鸡的时候你去碰她她会咬你的手，因为他要保护她的小鸡。然后你的爷爷那个时候，在那里旁边—洗那个咸菜。因为一边是做那个午饭，那个真正的，用锅里煮的。他要洗咸菜，一边洗咸菜一边就会唱那个歌。在一个很脏很乱，地上都是泥土的一个房间里。很小的一个房间。"


"When I hear this song I immediately think of the kitchen in my old home. Inside that kitchen was a firewood burner. It used branches cut from outside to stoke the fire and you’d cook below it. It was only like this that your grandma could cook. And you would cook alongside hot steam. And then next to this stove was a chicken cage. That is, the chickens would hide to the side—they’d come back at night and live there. And next to the cage there was a platform with a nest for the hen to raise eggs and hatch chicks. If you touched her, she would bite your hand because she wanted to protect her chicks. And your grandpa at the time, would be next to them washing pickled vegetables. Because to one side was the place for making lunch—the proper kind, with a pot. He needed to wash the vegetables, and while he washed them he would sing that song. In a very dirty and messy room, where the ground was all dirt. A room that was very small. "

"At the time I was taking a TOFL class and staying up every night to do homework, and after doing homework I would talk to my classmate. He was kind of depressed back then, and because of this I was also unhappy. We would often call each other. And then we discovered this song that we felt was very healing—it had many similarities to our own experience. Because of that, we would often listen to it together. After everything was over, it felt like I had listened to it too many times. Everytime I hear this song now I recall the things we talked about."
